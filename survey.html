Some Key Insights:

Most respondents know what spyware is and consider it to be a type of malware that is installed on a device without the user's knowledge. A variety of people are responsible for installing and maintaining security software on respondents' computers, including administrators, IT professionals, and employees. The majority of respondents use Windows 10 or 11 on their primary Internet-connected devices, with a few using other versions of Windows or macOS. Most respondents use Windows Update occasionally, with a few setting it to update automatically. The majority of respondents have anti-spyware software installed on their computers, with a few unsure or indicating that they do not know. The most commonly mentioned anti-spyware software is McAfee Internet Security or Microsoft AntiSpyware, with a few respondents mentioning other software. Some respondents use firewall software, but others are unsure or do not know. A few respondents indicated that their computer's administration is monitoring their device all the time, while others indicated that this is not the case or are unsure. .
